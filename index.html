Hi, I received my doctorate degree from Harbin Engineering University in Mar. 2018 and I passed my oral defense in Dec. 2017, under the supervision of Prof. Chunguang Ma. During my doctoral program, I have been very fortunate to have worked with Prof. Steven Galbraith and Prof. Hong-Sheng Zhou, respectively, as a research assistant (and a long-term visiting P.h.D student) from Dec. 2015 - Oct. 2017.

Now, I am a postdoctoral research associate at Lancaster University from Jan. 2018 worked with Dr. Bingsheng Zhang and Dr. Jan Willemson on cryptographic e-voting and zero-knowledge proof, etc. My postdoctoral works with my advisors are one of part of Cybernetica and IOHK.

Research Interests: My primary research interests are in cryptographic protocols, secure distributed computing, and privacy-enhancing technologies, including lattice-based cryptography, multiparty computation, and blockchain consensus. Recently, specific topics include: compute and query on encrypted data, verifiable computation in outsourced environments, and password-based cryptography.

I am leaving on the job market and joining to Qingdao University. Qingdao is the place in which I was born.
